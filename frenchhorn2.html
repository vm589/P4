Brass instruments have been around for as long as ancient history has started to be recorded. The french horn or the "horn" has a sweet mellow sound and can be traced back to the 15th century where men used to make horns from animal shells as a form of signalling. One of the most significant developments in the history of the French horn occurred in the early 19th century led by Heinrich Stölzel and Friedrich Blühmel in Germany, advanced the modern instrument by allowing players to produce a wider range of notes by producing valves and remaking the instrument.
