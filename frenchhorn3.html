The French Horn has had many Variations throughout history. Lets look through a few!


A popular choice for beginners, this is a Single Horn and it comes in F
Originated and used in Vienna, Austria, the Vienna Horn is a single horn and have one slide per valve. Unlike other horns, this horn has a two-cylinder valves called as pumpenvalves.
This is a marching french horn. It comes in F and has half the tubing of a regular french horn introduced in 1950.
Probably the most unique instrument. Presented in 1897 this instrument features a 4th thumb valve slide that helps you switch key from B to F.
